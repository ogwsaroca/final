What to do with Natural Hair

2/26/2024 15

Embracing natural hair is about understanding its unique needs. Many African women opt for methods that prioritize moisture retention and protective styles, such as twist-outs, braids, and Bantu knots. Key practices include using sulfate-free shampoos, deep conditioning regularly, and sealing moisture with natural oils like shea butter and coconut oil. Co-washing (using conditioner only) is also popular for maintaining softness and reducing dryness. Styling techniques such as the "big chop" have become a rite of passage in the natural hair journey, helping to transition away from chemically treated hair​

What to do with Afro Bulk

3/15/2024 80

Managing afro bulk involves techniques that keep the hair hydrated and minimize tangles. After washing, it’s recommended to detangle hair in sections using a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to prevent breakage. Incorporating leave-in conditioners and styling creams can define curls and control frizz, especially when styling into curly afros or sleek buns. For those looking to switch up their look, protective styles like box braids or twists can be effective in giving the hair a break while maintaining its volume.

How to Take Care of Dreadlocks

4/15/2024 3

Maintaining dreadlocks requires a balance of cleanliness and minimal manipulation. It’s important to keep the scalp clean by washing with a residue-free shampoo, as build-up can cause itching and dryness. To keep the locks firm and tidy, regular palm-rolling or interlocking can help maintain their shape. Hydration is key—applying natural oils like jojoba or argan oil can keep both the scalp and locks moisturized. As dreadlocks mature,​hat might lead to thinning or breakage.
